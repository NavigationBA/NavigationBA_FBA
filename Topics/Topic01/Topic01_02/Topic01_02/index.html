    Topic 1.2 - 商业分析师的核心技能¶

    1. 商业科学家的角色¶

    (1) 商业科学家的技能预期¶

    Expectations of business analytics:

    • How to write code
    • How to build charts
    • How to fit models

    Reality: Using data to tackle messy, ambiguous, and important business problems that requires:

    • Judgement
    • Sharp, answerable questions
    • Knowing which tools to use — and when

    (2) 商业科学家的思维模式¶

    Doing business analytics means thinking before coding:

    • Understand context
    • Frame the problem
    • Choose between different approaches

    A business scientist needs to:

    • Blend data, domain knowledge, and structured reasoning to guide better decisions
    • This has direct implications for their skill set and their skill acquisition

    2. 商业科学家的八项核心技能¶

    (1) 制度细节：理解商业世界 - Institutional Detail¶

    Businesses don't operate in a vacuum, so we need to understand:

    • Customers, competitors, suppliers, regulations, industry norms
    • How the organization works

    Why it matters:

    • Avoids misinterpreting the data
    • Helps frame better questions
    • Reveals meaningful patterns — and filters out misleading ones

    Institutional detail is not a distraction - it sharpens analysis

    (2) 数据发现：找到正确的数据 - Data Discovery¶

    Real-world data is messy, scattered, and incomplete

    • For example: spreadsheets, dashboards, databases, websites, even emails
    • We need to combine technical know-how with social intelligence to find what's useful

    • We need to ask:

      • What gets measured?
      • What doesn't get measured, and why?

    Sometimes, the data you want doesn't exist. Find workarounds:

    • Use proxies
    • Collect new data
    • Reframe the question

    (3) 商业理论：理解人和企业 - Business Theory¶

    We need to understand the motivations, incentives, and constraints behind decisions.

    Business theory:

    • Gives you mental models for making sense of patterns
    • Helps move from what happened → why it happened → what might happen next
    • Frames data through concepts from management strategy, consumer psychology, and economics

    Implication: Business scientists:

    • Think in trade-offs, incentives, and feedback loops
    • Know that behavior is often patterned and interpretable

    (4) 分析建模：选择正确的工具 - Analytical Modelling¶

    Business scientists match tool complexity to task complexity.

    Key questions to ask:

    • Do we need a descriptive or predictive or causal estimate?
    • How do we balance accuracy vs interpretability?

    The best model is the one that fits the question, the data, and the decision context.

    Sometimes, no model is the right choice:

    • A table or chart may tell the story best
    • Judgment matters: not every question is ready for modelling

    (5) 分析理论：了解可能出错的地方 - Analytical Theory¶

    Misleading analysis leads to bad decisions.

    Business scientists need to:

    • Think critically about what the data really shows
    • Question their own models and conclusions
    • Understand what their analysis can and can't say

    This is a version of statistical thinking:

    • Are we measuring what we think we are?
    • Could something else explain this pattern?
    • Would the conclusion change with different data?
    • What assumptions are we making — and are they reasonable?

    Being skeptical isn't being negative

    • It makes your work trustworthy
    • It not just makes your work impressive

    (6) 计算技能：使用代码和技术 - Computational Skills¶

    Today's business scientists work with code:

    • Tools like R to make analysis faster, clearer, and more reproducible
    • This isn't about becoming a software engineer
    • It's about solving data problems efficiently

    Modern business analytics spans:

    • Spreadsheets, databases, dashboards, cloud tools, models
    • Often different and continually newer platforms/models, but
    • Consistent mindset: Break problems into steps, fix what breaks, and automate the rest

    (7) 展示与沟通：传达发现 - Presentation & Communication¶

    Business scientists shape findings into clear, purposeful stories:

    • Tailor to your audience: executives want clarity, analysts want detail
    • Use graphs, slides, and writing to highlight what matters
    • Focus on what the data or model shows and what to do about it

    Good communication means:

    • Clean visuals
    • Clear structure
    • Sharp recommendations

    The goal isn't to impress, but to help decision makers think clearly and act.

    (8) 创造力与判断力：提出正确的问题 - Creativity & Judgment¶

    Business scientists:

    • Turn vague goals into clear, testable questions
    • Use creativity to reframe problems
    • Use judgment to focus on what matters

    You won't always get a clean brief:

    • Sometimes the question is too broad
    • Sometimes the data leads somewhere unexpected

    The best analysts act like investigators: curious, focused, and grounded in business relevance.

    3. 技能的应用与组合¶

    (1) 通用技能与情境技能¶

    Not every business scientist uses every skill equally.

    Some skills are universal:

    • Understanding the business world (Institutional Detail)
    • Finding the right data (Data Discovery)
    • Communicating what you find (Presentation & Communication)
    • Asking the right questions (Creativity & Judgment)

    Other skills are context-dependent:

    • Understanding people and firms (Business Theory)
    • Choosing the right tool (Analytical Modelling)
    • Knowing what can go wrong (Analytical Theory)
    • Working with code and technology (Computational Skills)

    The goal isn't to master everything, but to know what matters most for the problems you're solving.

    (2) 技能的构建¶

    You can't master all eight skills at once. But you can build them over time.

    Grow through:

    • Projects: real problems build real skill
    • Courses: pick what fits your goals
    • Collaboration: learn from others
    • Reflection: ask what worked, what didn't

    Key mindset:

    • Stay curious
    • Stay self-aware
    • Keep learning

    4. AI 时代的商业分析 - 为什么人类判断仍然重要¶

    AI can (usually) write code, generate charts, and surface patterns.

    However, AI can't:

    • Understand business context
    • Judge what matters
    • Navigate politics or ambiguity
    • Communicate with nuance

    Implication:

    • AI can help spot patterns, but only people know what they mean, why they matter, and what to do next

    • Use AI as a tool, not a replacement

    • Good analysts use AI to work faster, not to think less.
